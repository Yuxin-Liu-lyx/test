Average weekly income in Australia

This is a dashboard of average weekly income in Australia.
Designed by Yuxin Liu.



The Average weekly income in Australia visualisation explores the average weekly earnings in Australia from November 2020 to May 2021. The visualisation takes a starting point of per capita income by state, average male income, average female income and average industry income to visualise the average weekly income in Australia.


Overall weekly income per capita in Australia

This is an choropleth map projection that shows the the per capita income of different regions of Australia. The brightness of the blue in the map reflects the per capita income of the region, with darker colours indicating higher per capita incomes.

We can see that the Australian Capital Territory is the deepest blue, although this area is small and not easily visible. This is followed by the Western Australian region. This indicates that the Australian Capital Territory is the region with the highest weekly income per capita, which is $1980.6, and Western Australian is the region with the second highest weekly income per capita in Australia, which is $1879.7


Comparison of average weekly earnings of Male and Female in different industry

This is a Bubble Plot, the chart divides the 18 industries into four categories, with the categories differentiated by four colours. From this plot, we can see that the average weekly earnings in different industry and thus further compare the earnings in different industry and the earnings of Male and Female in different industry.

This plot reflects that the highest paying industry is mining, with both male and female earning a much higher average weekly income than other industries, with male earning an average of $2,748.7 and female $2,276.8.


Comparison of weekly per capita income in the private and public sectors

This Box plot shows the income of the public and private sectors for male female and per capita respectively. Blue represents public and orange represents private, and by placing the mouse over the colour blocks you can see the highest, lowest and median values of the data.

The plot reflects the fact that Male have the highest income levels in the public sector and Female have the lowest income levels in the private sector.

This plot clearly reflects the fact that female earn less than male, which indicates that female generally suffer from some inequalities in the workplace.

Distribution of Happiness Score across Different Regions

By looking at this area map, it can be seen that Australia's weekly income per capita has continued to increase over the last decade. However by 20202, this trend slows down and arguably even disappears.
